Bio

I am a PhD student in Operations Research at Imperial College London, advised by Prof. Wolfram Wiesemann and Prof. Ryan Cory-Wright. My research interest lies in stochastic, robust, and distributionally robust optimization, as well as their applications in financial risk and supply chain management. Before joining Imperial, I obtained my Master’s degree from EPFL, Switzerland, where I was awarded the Excellence Fellowship. While in Lausanne, I worked on multiple research projects across machine learning robustness, multi-armed bandits, and distributionally robust optimization. Besides, I spent 6 months as a research intern at Logitech Europe S.A, where I worked on learning-based signal processing. Before that, I obtained my Bachelor’s degree from Xi’an Jiaotong University.
